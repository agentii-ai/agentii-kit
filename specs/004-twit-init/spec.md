### The 80/20 Rule (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Principle**: 80% of posts must provide genuine value; only 20% can be promotional.

**Value-Driven Content Examples**:
- Educational threads teaching financial analysis or spec-driven development
- Data visualizations and market insights
- Industry problem identification (pain points, bottlenecks)
- Genuine insights from domain expertise
- Behind-the-scenes "build in public" transparency

**Promotional Content Examples** (max 20% of posts):
- Feature announcements tied to customer problems
- Beta invitations with clear value proposition
- Case studies showing quantified results
- Product demonstrations with compelling narratives

**Monthly Audit**: If > 20% of posts are promotional, rebalance immediately. Quality of engagement > quantity of posts.

### Thread Mastery Formula

**Structure** (for major threads, 2 per week per account):
1. **Hook (Tweet 1)**: Bold statement or curiosity gap
   - Example: "I analyzed 500 earnings calls and found this one AI pattern every investor missed"
2. **Setup (Tweets 2-3)**: Establish problem or context, show why this matters
3. **Value Delivery (Tweets 4-10)**: 1-5 concrete, actionable insights (numbered: 1/, 2/, 3/)
4. **Proof/Social Proof (Tweet 11-12)**: Metrics, customer results, benchmarks
5. **Call-to-Action (Final Tweet)**: "Follow for daily AI financial research insights" or "RT if you've faced this problem"

**Optimization**:
- Post threads at 8 AM ET Tuesday-Thursday (peak financial professional hours)
- Within 60 minutes: share to LinkedIn as carousel
- At 24-hour mark: quote tweet with new angle
- Convert to blog post within 48 hours (SEO and email list building)

## Part 8: Constraints & Risks

### Technical Constraints

**Platform Limits**:
- Twitter rate limits: Follows (20-30/day max), Replies (10-15/day max), DMs (5-10/day max)
- Algorithm favors native video over links (embed demos, don't link to YouTube)
- Thread length: optimal 10-15 tweets (longer threads see drop-off)

**Resource Constraints**:
- **Time**: 2-3 hours/day total for both accounts (1.5 hrs @agentii_ai, 0.5-1 hr @frank_agentii)
- **Content Creation**: Need 3-5 visual assets/week (screen recordings, charts, diagrams)
- **Engagement**: 30 min/day per account for replies, 30 min/day for strategic engagement

### Strategic Risks

**Risk 1: Over-Promotional Content Kills Engagement**
- **Mitigation**: Monthly content audit enforcing 80/20 rule (80% value, 20% promotion)
- **Validation**: If engagement rate drops below 1.5%, immediately rebalance content mix

**Risk 2: Inconsistent Posting Destroys Algorithmic Momentum**
- **Mitigation**: Use scheduling tools (Buffer, Typefully, HypeFury) for consistency
- **Validation**: Maintain 1-3 posts/day minimum; track algorithmic reach weekly

**Risk 3: Buying Followers or Aggressive Automation Damages Brand**
- **Mitigation**: Zero tolerance for purchased followers or automated replies without human approval
- **Validation**: Follower quality audit monthly (50%+ engaged followers required)

**Risk 4: Launching Without Sufficient Audience (< 2,000 followers)**
- **Mitigation**: If Week 12 target not met, delay launch by 2-4 weeks and double engagement efforts
- **Validation**: Pre-launch checklist: 3,000+ combined followers, 500+ waitlist, 3-5 influencer commitments

**Risk 5: Single-Channel Launch Limits Reach**
- **Mitigation**: Coordinate Twitter with email, LinkedIn, blog, Hacker News, Reddit simultaneously
- **Validation**: Launch day checklist: Twitter threads posted, blog posts published, email sent, HN submitted, LinkedIn cross-posted

**Risk 6: Product-Market Fit Not Achieved (Twitter Traffic Doesn't Convert)**
- **Mitigation**: If conversion rate < 2% in first 30 days, revisit messaging, landing page, or product positioning
- **Validation**: Weekly cohort analysis showing beta signup â†’ trial â†’ paid conversion funnel

### Compliance & Ethical Constraints

**Financial Disclosure Requirements** (for @agentii_ai):
- All investment analysis must include disclaimers: "Educational only, not financial advice"
- Author disclosure: Any personal holdings mentioned
- Risk acknowledgment: Mandatory section addressing downside scenarios
- Prohibited: "Can't miss" language, unnamed sources, extrapolating single-quarter results to multi-year trends

**Authenticity Standards** (for @frank_agentii):
- No survivorship bias ("successful founders all do X" without acknowledging failures)
- Failure case inclusion: Every "how we did X" post includes "what we tried that didn't work"
- Numbers & metrics: Share actual data (velocity, CAC, churn) not just anecdotes
- Prohibited: Generic advice without personal validation, treating founders as passive recipients
